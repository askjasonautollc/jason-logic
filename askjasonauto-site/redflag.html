Jason’s Smart Buyer Checklist

“If it smells like lies and knocks like debt, walk.” – Jason

New to buying or flipping cars? This checklist walks you through every major red flag—Jason-style—and gives you a space to track what you find.

1. “It Just Needs a Sensor”

🔧 If a seller says this, it usually means they're hiding something. Don’t assume it's cheap or simple.

Ask: “What sensor exactly? Have you had it diagnosed?”

Note any warning lights you see: ___________________________

If you have a scanner: Plug in and scan for codes—most auto parts stores will do this for free.

  • No scanner? Ask to drive it to AutoZone or O’Reilly—most will check for free.
  • If there are codes, look them up online for your car’s year/make/model.
  • Be wary if they say it was "just cleared" or recently reset.
  • More than one code = more than one problem.
Notes:

2. “No Title on Me”

📄 No title match = walk. That’s not a deal. That’s a risk.

  • Match title name to license ID
  • Check for open reassignments (back of title)
  • Use VIN to verify title and lien status
  • Don’t accept “I’m selling for a friend”
Notes:

3. “Check Engine Light’s Always On”

🚨 The oldest excuse. Could be emissions, misfire, evap leak, or worse.

  • Scan for codes and readiness monitors
  • Ask if it recently passed emissions or inspection
  • If monitors aren’t ready, codes were cleared
  • Estimate repair cost before you buy
Notes:

4. “It Drives Fine But…”

🛞 Always test drive. Let the car prove it’s solid.

  • Drive 55–65 mph. Listen for vibration or hum
  • Brake test: it shouldn’t pull or pulsate
  • Check all tires for wear patterns
  • Look under the car after the drive—new leaks?
Notes:

5. “Clean Title, Just Cosmetic”

🚘 Paint hides damage. Don’t get duped.

  • Check all panel gaps: hood, trunk, doors
  • Look for overspray on trim and seals
  • Use a magnet or paint meter to find filler
  • Ask if airbags have ever been deployed
Notes:

Seller Script: What to Say

“I just want to make a smart buy. Can I ask you a few quick things?”

  • “Is the title in your name and here with the car?”
  • “Do you know what code the CEL is throwing?”
  • “Any crash history or airbag deployment?”
  • “If it all checks out, what’s your best cash price?”
